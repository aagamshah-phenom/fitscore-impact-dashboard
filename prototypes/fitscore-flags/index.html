Scoring configuration NEW
Location proximity threshold
When enabled, candidates beyond the specified distance from the job location — or whose location cannot be determined — cannot receive an A FitScore. They may still receive B, C, or No Fit scores normally.
How this works: Set a maximum distance below. Any candidate whose parsed profile location falls outside that radius from the job site will be capped at B FitScore. Candidates with no parseable location are also capped. Candidates within the radius are scored normally across all bands. This setting applies tenant-wide to all jobs with a specified location.
Maximum distance

Candidates located more than 50 miles from the job site, or whose location cannot be determined from their profile, will not receive an A FitScore.

Lock FitScore after first assignment
When enabled, a candidate's FitScore for a job is locked once it is first assigned. It will not change if the candidate updates their profile or uploads a new resume. Only changes to the job's matching criteria will trigger a rescore.
Why this matters: By default, FitScores recalculate whenever a candidate updates their profile on an open job. Locking scores ensures recruiters compare candidates on a consistent baseline throughout the hiring process.
Disable years of experience
When enabled, years of experience carries zero weight in FitScore calculations. Candidates are evaluated on skills, title alignment, and location only. When off, years of experience is factored in normally.
Important: This setting takes effect for new scoring events only. Scores already assigned before this change are not recalculated. Applies tenant-wide to all jobs. Enable this to reduce experience-based bias or open roles to career changers evaluated primarily on skills.
