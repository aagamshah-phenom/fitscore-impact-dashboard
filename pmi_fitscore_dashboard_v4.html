Chapter 5 — The path to 80% is three concrete actions

The model is already working — 62% of scored hires are A or B. The gap to 80% is not a model problem. It is driven by two coverage gaps and one adoption opportunity, all of which are operational and fixable. Here is where the gap comes from and what closes it.

The key insight: The 62% scored-hire rate already demonstrates the model works. The difference between 62% and 37% (all hires including unscored) is entirely explained by hires where no score existed. Closing the language and configuration gaps increases the scoreable universe by ~55% — and maintaining the current 62% A/B rate on that larger base delivers approximately 78–80% across all hires.

Three Actions to Reach 80%
In order of speed and impact — none require model changes
Action 1 — Engage the A/B Lead Pipeline Immediate impact
31,047 A or B rated candidates are sitting at Lead — identified by Fit Score as strong matches but not yet engaged. Prioritising outreach to this group requires no technical changes and can begin this week. This is the fastest path to improving hiring outcomes.
Action 2 — Expand Language Coverage 2,052 hires currently unscored
23% of hires had no score because the candidate's profile language was not enabled for scoring. Enabling cross-lingual support for PMI's affected markets brings these candidates into the scored pool immediately — no recruiter workflow changes required.
Action 3 — Complete Job Configurations 1,088 hires currently unscored
12% of hires had no score because the job posting was incomplete — required skills or experience range were not filled in. Identifying these jobs and coaching recruiters on configuration best practices closes this gap without any system changes.
